# 📅 Rhythms & Rituals

> *"Continuous discovery is a cadence — a weekly heartbeat that keeps you connected to customers and evidence."*
> — Teresa Torres (aligned with Cagan's model)

Good PM work is not reactive. It is rhythmic. The cadences below are the repeating structures that keep discovery, delivery, outcomes, and communication running without relying on willpower or memory.

---

## Daily

**Discovery:**
- Monitor experiments, especially recently launched ones — any early signals?
- Check product analytics (PostHog) for anything unusual
- Review any new user feedback, complaints, or support tickets

**Delivery:**
- Check progress of current sprint tickets — anything blocked?
- Ensure engineers know what to pick up next
- Respond to any blocking questions from the squad

**Communication:**
- Process stakeholder requests — log them, acknowledge receipt

---

## Weekly

**Discovery:**
- 1-2 user interviews (CF lawyers or OS users — non-guided observation)
- After each interview: extract learnings, update Opportunity Solution Tree
- Create or review 2-3 experiments for riskiest current assumptions
- Review the end-to-end customer journey for new opportunities
- Spend 30-60 min using the product as a user would

**Outcomes:**
- Review current OKR metrics — are we moving the needle?
- Check experiment results for outcome signals

**Delivery:**
- Backlog refinement: ensure the next sprint's tickets are well-defined
- Write or review user stories for upcoming work
- Update roadmap to reflect current reality (what shipped, what moved, what's next)

**Communication:**
- Send weekly product update to squad and stakeholders
- Post-release: send release notes to lawyers and internal OS users
- Present in Product Weekly: what you worked on, key updates, what you learned

---

## Bi-Weekly

- Squad check-in: team health, blockers, morale
- OKR check-in: are we on track? What needs to change?
- Present what we shipped to stakeholders (demo or written update)

---

## Monthly

- Sync on OKR progress — deeper review with management
- Competitive landscape review (capped at 1 hour — don't let this eat your calendar)
- Adjust roadmap if strategy requires it
- Review and clean the backlog — remove stale items, re-prioritise

---

## Quarterly

- **OKR Planning:**
  - Retrospective on the closing quarter — what moved? What didn't? What did we learn?
  - Align with leadership on next quarter's Product Goals and Outcomes
  - Set OKRs with baselines and targets
  - Draft the Now / Next / Later roadmap for the new quarter

- **Strategy Review:**
  - Is the vision still right?
  - What is the single biggest strategic bet for this quarter?
  - What are we explicitly not doing?

- **Stakeholder alignment:**
  - Present the new quarter's direction to stakeholders
  - Surface any cross-functional dependencies early

---

## Weekly Review: Critical Questions

Use these at the end of each week as a structured reflection — in a 15-30 minute solo review or with the squad.

**Discovery & Learning**
- What did we learn this week?
- What new user problems, needs, or opportunities did we uncover?
- What user feedback did we review — interviews, tickets, Slack, sales, support?

**Assumptions & Experiments**
- Which assumptions did we work on?
- Which experiments did we launch — and what did they tell us?
- What was validated? What was invalidated?

**Delivery**
- What did we ship this week?
- What meaningful progress did we make toward our current outcome?

**Outcomes & Analytics**
- What metrics did we look at?
- Did we move any outcomes — or fail to move them?
- What does the data say we should double down on, change, or stop?

**Reflection & Forward Look**
- What blocked or slowed us down?
- What are the top priorities for next week?
- What do we most need to learn next?
