# ⚡ Productivity

> *"The way you spend your time is the strategy. Not what you say you'll do — what you actually do."*

Productivity for a PM is not about doing more. It is about protecting the time and focus needed to do the right things well. This page defines how to allocate time, structure the week, and build habits that compound.

---

## Time Allocation

A week is roughly 40 hours. Here is how to split it across the five pillars:

| Pillar | Allocation | Hours/week | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔍 Discovery | **30%** | ~12h | The single highest-leverage activity. Most value is destroyed by building the wrong thing. |
| 🚢 Delivery | **25%** | ~10h | Enabling engineering to move fast, stay unblocked, and ship with quality. |
| 📊 Outcomes & Analytics | **25%** | ~10h | Knowing whether anything you built actually moved the needle. |
| 📣 Communication | **10%** | ~4h | Alignment with stakeholders and leadership — enough to stay trusted, not more. |
| ♠️ Strategy | **10%** | ~4h | Direction-setting, roadmap planning, and saying no. Needs space, but not constant attention. |

**Notes:**
- These percentages shift with product maturity and team phase. Early stage → more discovery. Scaling phase → more delivery and outcomes.
- Communication tends to inflate. Watch for it. Alignment is a tool, not a goal.
- Strategy time is rarely "meetings" — it is often thinking, writing, and reading. Protect it.

## Daily Habits (~30–60 min)

### Discovery
- [ ] Monitor active experiments (especially new launches)
- [ ] Check product analytics for insights or anomalies
- [ ] Review customer feedback (support tickets, complaints)
- [ ] Schedule interviews if none planned this week

### Delivery
- [ ] Review sprint board — what's blocked or at risk?
- [ ] Unblock developers with decisions or clarifications
- [ ] Ensure devs know what to work on next

### Communication
- [ ] After releases: create release notes and notify stakeholders
- [ ] Quick Slack updates on key decisions or blockers

---

## Weekly Habits (~8–12 hours)

### Discovery (3–4 hours)
- [ ] Conduct 1–2 user interviews (30–45 min each)
- [ ] After interviews: update the Opportunity-Solution Tree
  - Extract key learnings
  - Break down problems into specific opportunities
  - Form assumptions on solutions
- [ ] Active solution candidate(s) identified — always at least one solution being actively explored
- [ ] Riskiest assumptions for current solution(s) made explicit and ranked
- [ ] At least one solution-focused test running or completed (prototype, usability test, concierge test)
- [ ] Experiment results reviewed — what changes as a result?
- [ ] Cross-functional check: Design and Engineering aligned on current solution direction
- [ ] Product analytics checked for new signals
- [ ] Support tickets and customer complaints reviewed
- [ ] Spend time using the product as a user would

### Outcomes & Analytics (2 hours)
- [ ] Review current Product Outcomes with the squad
- [ ] Check OKR progress and leading indicators
- [ ] Analyze experiment results and document learnings

### Delivery (2–3 hours)
- [ ] Write and refine user stories (clear problem, AC, priority, outcome alignment)
- [ ] Backlog refinement: plan next sprint(s)
- [ ] Sprint ceremonies (planning, review, retro) as applicable
- [ ] Release management if applicable

### Communication (1 hour)
- [ ] Product Weekly meeting — share what you worked on, what you learned, key updates
- [ ] Team sync on priorities and blockers
- [ ] Stakeholder updates post-release

---

## Bi-Weekly Habits (~1–2 hours)

- [ ] Squad check-in (team health, morale, process)
- [ ] OKR progress review with team
- [ ] Present what shipped to stakeholders
- [ ] Deep-dive analytics review

---

## Monthly Habits (~2–4 hours)

- [ ] Synthesize patterns across all discovery activities
- [ ] OKR progress review with leadership
- [ ] Roadmap review — do we need strategic adjustments?
- [ ] Team retrospective on process and collaboration
- [ ] All-hands product update

---

## Quarterly Habits (~8–16 hours)

- [ ] Score and close out previous quarter's OKRs
- [ ] Quarterly retrospective: what worked, what didn't, key learnings
- [ ] Plan next quarter's Product Objectives and Key Results
- [ ] Align with leadership on strategic priorities
- [ ] Roadmap planning session
- [ ] Update product strategy documentation
- [ ] Team planning and capacity review

---

## Weekly Reflection Questions

Use these in your weekly review to check that time is being spent on the right things:

**Discovery & Learning**
- What did I learn this week?
- What new user problems or opportunities did I uncover?
- What surprising insights emerged?

**Assumptions & Experiments**
- Which assumptions did I work on this week?
- Which experiments launched or completed?
- What did the results tell us? What changed?

**Delivery**
- What meaningful progress did we make toward a solution?
- What's blocked or at risk?

**Outcomes & Analytics**
- What metrics did I look at?
- Did anything move? Why or why not?
- Are we on track for our OKRs?

**Reflection**
- What slowed me down or blocked me?
- What decisions do I still need to make?
- What should I say no to next week?
- What do I want to learn next?
