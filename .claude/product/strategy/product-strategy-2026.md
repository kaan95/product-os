## Strategic Pillars (How We Win)

### Pillar 1: **Platform Orchestration** [Core Business Model]

**What**: We orchestrate the entire legal case lifecycle across three stakeholders (RSV, lawyers, clients) through a unified digital platform

**Why It Matters**: Legal cases fail due to fragmentation - disconnected intake, unclear communication, unpredictable workflows. We eliminate friction by orchestrating the entire journey.

**How We'll Do It**:
1. **Structured Case Intake** → Standardized intake forms, automated data capture, intelligent routing
2. **Intelligent Lawyer Matching** → Algorithm-based matching on expertise, availability, performance, case characteristics
3. **Mandate Distribution** → Optimized distribution ensuring balanced workload, quality, and margin optimization
4. **Multi-Stakeholder Communication** → Unified communication hub between client, lawyer, and RSV
5. **Case Workflow Orchestration** → Standardized workflows with defined SLAs and status tracking

**Success Metrics**:
- Case intake → lawyer assignment: <24 hours (vs. 3-5 days industry standard)
- Case resolution time: 30% faster than traditional model
- Stakeholder satisfaction: >80% NPS from all three sides
- Platform coverage: 70%+ of RSV partner cases flow through platform

**Investment**: 40% of R&D resources in 2026

**Strategic Importance**: This IS our business - without orchestration, we're just software

---

### Pillar 2: **AI-Powered Automation** [Competitive Differentiator]

**What**: Use AI to automate repetitive, time-consuming tasks that don't require human judgment

**Why It Matters**: Manual work doesn't scale and creates bottlenecks. AI automation enables 3x throughput without proportional headcount growth.

**How We'll Do It**:
1. **Document Processing AI** → Auto-classify inbound documents, extract key data, route to right stakeholder
2. **Invoice Automation AI** → Generate accurate invoices from case data, reducing finance team burden
3. **Lawyer Assistance AI** → Suggest case actions, draft routine documents, auto-respond to common inquiries
4. **Coverage Check AI** → Automatically validate RSV coverage, flag edge cases for human review

**Success Metrics**:
- Document processing: <12 hours (baseline: 5.6 days)
- Invoice automation: 90% fully automated (baseline: 50%)
- Lawyer efficiency: 30% time savings on routine tasks
- Coverage check accuracy: 95%+ (baseline: manual review)

**Investment**: 35% of R&D resources in 2026

**Strategic Importance**: AI is the **enabler** of platform scale, not the product itself

---

### Pillar 3: **Process Standardization & Quality Assurance** [Trust Builder]

**What**: Define standard processes, SLAs, and quality gates for all case workflows

**Why It Matters**: Legal service quality today is unpredictable - depends on which lawyer you get. Standardization makes quality **measurable and improvable**.

**How We'll Do It**:
1. **Process Definition** → Document standard workflows for each case type (traffic, employment, housing, etc.)
2. **SLA Enforcement** → Define and track SLAs (first contact <24hrs, case updates every 7 days, etc.)
3. **Quality Gates** → Checkpoints that must be met before case progresses (coverage confirmed, client informed, documents submitted)
4. **Performance Measurement** → Track lawyer performance (resolution time, client satisfaction, compliance)
5. **Continuous Improvement** → Use data to identify bottlenecks and optimize processes

**Success Metrics**:
- SLA compliance: 90%+ across all case types
- Lawyer performance visibility: 100% of lawyers have performance dashboards
- Process compliance: 95%+ of cases follow standard workflows
- Quality consistency: <10% variance in case outcomes by lawyer

**Investment**: 10% of R&D resources in 2026 (process design + tooling)

**Strategic Importance**: This is what RSV customers **pay for** - predictable, high-quality service delivery

---

### Pillar 4: **Data-Driven Infrastructure** [Strategic Moat]

**What**: Build comprehensive data foundation that makes legal services transparent, measurable, and optimizable

**Why It Matters**: Legal market lacks data - decisions are made on intuition, not evidence. Data foundation enables **intelligent steering** of the platform.

**How We'll Do It**:
1. **Case Data Capture** → Structure all case data (intake, actions, communications, outcomes)
2. **Performance Analytics** → Real-time dashboards for RSV, lawyers, and internal teams
3. **Predictive Insights** → ML models to predict case duration, costs, success likelihood
4. **Margin Optimization** → Data-driven mandate distribution to optimize profitability
5. **Quality Control** → Automated detection of at-risk cases (missed SLAs, low satisfaction, etc.)

**Success Metrics**:
- Data completeness: 95%+ of cases have full structured data
- Predictive accuracy: 85%+ accuracy on case duration predictions
- Platform intelligence: Automated recommendations accepted 70%+ of time
- Business impact: 20% margin improvement through intelligent distribution

**Investment**: 15% of R&D resources in 2026

**Strategic Importance**: Data moat = defensible competitive advantage. More cases = better data = smarter platform = win more RSV customers.

## Strategic Trade-offs & Decisions

### Trade-off 1: **Platform Scale vs. Platform Quality**

**Decision**: Prioritize quality over growth in Phase 1-2, then scale aggressively in Phase 3

**Rationale**:
- ✅ Bad experience for one RSV partner kills referrals → can't grow
- ✅ Legal services have zero tolerance for errors → quality is existential
- ✅ Better to onboard 5 RSV partners with 95% satisfaction than 20 with 70% satisfaction
- ❌ Slower growth in Year 1, may cede first-mover advantage to competitors

**How We'll Measure**: If RSV NPS <80 or complaint rate >2%, pause new RSV onboarding until fixed.

---

### Trade-off 2: **AI Accuracy vs. Speed to Market**

**Decision**: Don't ship AI features until 90%+ accuracy validated in pilot

**Rationale**:
- ✅ Legal documents are high-stakes - one major error destroys trust
- ✅ RSV customers are risk-averse - need proof of reliability
- ✅ Better to launch Q2 with 95% accuracy than Q1 with 85% accuracy
- ❌ Competitors may ship faster with lower quality

**How We'll Enforce**: Mandatory 2-week pilot with 10% of users, accuracy review before scale.

---

### Trade-off 3: **Breadth (All Case Types) vs. Depth (Key Case Types Done Well)**

**Decision**: Start with 3 high-volume case types (traffic, employment, housing), expand later

**Rationale**:
- ✅ These 3 types = 70% of RSV case volume → highest impact
- ✅ Deep expertise in 3 types > shallow coverage of 20 types
- ✅ Can validate platform model before expanding to long-tail
- ❌ Some RSV partners want full case type coverage → may lose deals

**Expansion Plan**: Add 2-3 new case types per quarter in Phase 2, reaching 10+ types by end of 2026.

---

### Trade-off 4: **Build vs. Buy (Technology)**

**Decision**: Build core platform orchestration, buy commoditized features

**Rationale**:
- ✅ **Build**: Case orchestration, matching algorithm, mandate distribution = core IP
- ✅ **Buy**: Calendar scheduling, video calls, document storage = commodity features
- ✅ Faster time to market, lower technical risk
- ❌ Some vendor lock-in for non-core features

**Strategic Rule**: If it's our moat (orchestration, data, matching), we build. If it's commodity, we buy.

---

### Trade-off 5: **RSV-Centric vs. Multi-Sided Platform**

**Decision**: RSV-centric in 2026 (Phase 1-2), multi-sided in 2027+ (Phase 3)

**Rationale**:
- ✅ RSV partners are our customers - must solve their problems first
- ✅ Lawyers will adopt tools if RSV partners demand it (top-down adoption)
- ✅ Simpler go-to-market - sell to 50 RSV partners vs. 50,000 lawyers
- ❌ Leaves lawyer-side revenue on table in Year 1

**Expansion Trigger**: Once 10+ RSV partners are satisfied (NPS >80), introduce lawyer-side SaaS tools.

## Strategic Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: **RSV Partner Churn** 🔴 CRITICAL

**Impact**: If we lose 1-2 major RSV partners, platform collapses (both sides of marketplace disappear)

**Likelihood**: Medium (if we don't deliver on quality promises)

**Root Causes**:
- Poor case handling experience
- Slow resolution times
- Opaque processes
- Higher costs than traditional model

**Mitigation**:
- Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs) with every RSV partner - measure satisfaction, address issues
- Contractual SLAs with penalty clauses - we're accountable to metrics
- White-glove onboarding - dedicate Customer Success Manager to each new RSV partner
- Early warning system - automated alerts if RSV satisfaction drops below threshold

**Contingency Plan**: If RSV partner threatens to churn, escalate to CEO, offer custom solutions, discount pricing.

---

### Risk 2: **Lawyer Supply-Side Quality Issues** 🔴 CRITICAL

**Impact**: If lawyer quality is inconsistent, RSV partners lose trust and leave platform

**Likelihood**: Medium-High (legal service quality is inherently variable)

**Root Causes**:
- Insufficient lawyer vetting
- No performance tracking
- Weak enforcement of SLAs
- Lawyers treating platform as side project

**Mitigation**:
- Strict vetting process - only top 20% of applicants approved
- Performance dashboards - visible to lawyers and RSV partners
- Tiered lawyer system - high performers get more/better cases (gamification)
- Offboarding process - remove low-performing lawyers (<70% quality score)

**Contingency Plan**: If lawyer quality issues spike, pause new lawyer onboarding, tighten vetting standards.

---

### Risk 3: **AI Accuracy Below 90%** 🔴 CRITICAL

**Impact**: If AI doesn't hit 90% accuracy, users won't adopt it → entire automation strategy fails

**Likelihood**: Medium (no validation yet for document processing)

**Mitigation**:
- Pilot with 10% of users before scaling (see Trade-off 2)
- Human-in-the-loop workflows for edge cases
- Weekly accuracy reviews during pilot
- Accept that some workflows may need 85% accuracy + human review

**Contingency Plan**: If 90% proves unachievable, fall back to hybrid model (AI pre-sorts, human reviews).

---

### Risk 4: **Competitive Response** 🟡 HIGH

**Impact**: Incumbents (large law firms, practice management software vendors) launch competing platforms

**Likelihood**: Medium (legal tech is hot, but market is fragmented)

**Competitive Threats**:
- Large law firm networks launch their own platforms
- Practice management software (Clio, etc.) add RSV integrations
- New AI legal tech startups target RSV market
- RSV insurers build in-house platforms

**Mitigation**:
- **Network effects moat** - hard to replicate 10+ RSV + 5,000+ lawyers
- **Data moat** - more cases = better AI models = smarter platform
- **Integration moat** - deep RSV system integrations = high switching cost
- **Speed** - move fast in Phase 1-2 to lock in market before competitors wake up

**Strategic Response**: If major competitor launches, double down on quality and network effects, not feature parity.

---

### Risk 5: **Regulatory/Legal Risk** 🟡 MEDIUM

**Impact**: Legal practice regulations restrict platform model (e.g., lawyer independence requirements)

**Likelihood**: Low-Medium (legal market is heavily regulated)

**Regulatory Concerns**:
- Unauthorized practice of law (we must not practice law, only provide infrastructure)
- Lawyer independence (we can't dictate legal strategy, only process standards)
- Data privacy (GDPR compliance for sensitive case data)
- Insurance regulations (may restrict how RSV partners use platforms)

**Mitigation**:
- Legal counsel review of all platform features - ensure compliance
- Clear boundaries - we orchestrate process, lawyers make legal decisions
- GDPR-compliant architecture from day 1
- Regulatory engagement - proactively work with bar associations, regulators

**Contingency Plan**: If regulations change, adapt platform model to maintain compliance (e.g., looser process control).

## Open Strategic Questions

### 1. **How Do We Balance RSV Needs vs. Lawyer Needs When They Conflict?**

RSV partners want **lower costs** (pressure on lawyer fees).
Lawyers want **fair compensation** (higher fees for complex cases).

**Tension**: Optimizing for one side may alienate the other.

**Options**:
- **A**: RSV-first - optimize for RSV cost savings, accept some lawyer churn
- **B**: Balanced - find win-win pricing models that work for both
- **C**: Lawyer-first - ensure lawyer satisfaction, pass costs to RSV

**Recommendation**: **Option B (Balanced)** - Use data to show RSV that fair lawyer compensation = higher quality = fewer complaints = lower total cost. Win-win through efficiency, not cost-cutting.

**Next Step**: Model impact of lawyer fee structures on quality/satisfaction/costs by end of Q1.

---

### 2. **Should We Build for RSV Market Only, or Expand to Direct-to-Consumer?**

**Current Model**: B2B2C (serve RSV insurers who serve clients)

**Alternative**: B2C (serve clients directly, bypass RSV)

**Trade-offs**:
- **B2B2C (Current)**:
  - ✅ Higher ACV (€300K-€2M per RSV vs. €50-200 per client)
  - ✅ Lower CAC (sell to 50 RSV partners vs. 1M clients)
  - ✅ Predictable revenue (annual contracts)
  - ❌ Dependent on RSV market (limited TAM)

- **B2C (Alternative)**:
  - ✅ Massive TAM (millions of potential clients)
  - ✅ No middleman (keep full margin)
  - ❌ High CAC (consumer marketing is expensive)
  - ❌ Small transaction size (€50-200 per case)
  - ❌ Competes with our RSV customers

**Recommendation**: **Stay B2B2C for 2026-2027**, revisit B2C in 2028 if RSV market saturates.

**Next Step**: Monitor RSV market penetration - if we reach 30%+ market share, reassess.

---

### 3. **How Aggressive Should We Be on Pricing to Win Market Share?**

**Options**:
- **A**: Premium pricing (€200+ per case) - position as quality platform
- **B**: Market pricing (€100-150 per case) - match competitors
- **C**: Penetration pricing (€50-75 per case) - undercut to win share fast

**Trade-offs**:
- **A (Premium)**: Higher margin, slower growth, must prove ROI
- **B (Market)**: Balanced approach, compete on quality not price
- **C (Penetration)**: Fast growth, burn cash, raise capital

**Recommendation**: **Option B (Market Pricing) in Phase 1-2**, consider **Option C (Penetration)** in Phase 3 if we raise growth capital.

**Strategic Logic**: Need to prove platform works before competing on price. Once proven, can undercut with better unit economics.

**Next Step**: Benchmark competitor pricing and RSV willingness-to-pay by end of Q1.

---

### 4. **Do We Need to Own the Lawyer Network, or Can It Be Open?**

**Current Model**: Vetted partner lawyer network (curated supply side)

**Alternative**: Open marketplace (any lawyer can join, algorithms determine matching)

**Trade-offs**:
- **Curated (Current)**:
  - ✅ Quality control through vetting
  - ✅ Consistent experience for RSV partners
  - ✅ Platform liable for lawyer performance → strong incentive to ensure quality
  - ❌ Slower supply-side growth (vetting bottleneck)
  - ❌ Legal risk if we're seen as employer vs. platform

- **Open (Alternative)**:
  - ✅ Faster lawyer network growth
  - ✅ More competition = better prices for RSV
  - ❌ Quality highly variable (no vetting)
  - ❌ Poor experience destroys platform trust

**Recommendation**: **Curated network for 2026**, consider **hybrid model** (curated + open tiers) in 2027+.

**Strategic Logic**: Quality is existential in legal services. Can't risk bad lawyer experiences in Year 1. Once brand is established, can open up with tier system (verified vs. unverified lawyers).

**Next Step**: Monitor lawyer supply constraints - if vetting becomes bottleneck, pilot open tier with clear quality warnings.

---

### 5. **What Happens If Document Processing Stays at 5.6 Days?**

**Context**: Document processing (5.6 days) is labeled "Medium priority" in Jira but is **existential** for platform experience.

**Impact Analysis**:
- **If NOT solved in Q1**:
  - RSV partners experience delays in case progression
  - Lawyers frustrated by slow document routing
  - Clients complain about unresponsive service
  - Platform NPS drops → RSV churn risk increases

- **If solved (to <12 hours)**:
  - Case velocity increases 10x
  - Stakeholder satisfaction improves
  - Can handle 3x volume without ops headcount
  - Competitive advantage (no competitor has this)

**Recommendation**: **Immediately relabel OS-2713 as "Highest Priority"** and allocate 50% of AI Squad resources.

**Next Step**: This week - reprioritize initiatives to make document processing THE #1 focus for Q1.

- **Impact**: Can't scale to 10+ RSV partners without systematic onboarding
- **Recommendation**: Create Customer Success initiative or hire dedicated CS team

**Gap 3: Process Standardization Work Missing**
- **Issue**: No work on defining standard workflows, SLAs, or quality gates
- **Impact**: As we scale, quality will become inconsistent without standardized processes
- **Recommendation**: Allocate Product Manager to document processes + build enforcement tools

**Gap 4: Lawyer Performance & Quality Assurance Missing**
- **Issue**: No tracking of lawyer performance, no offboarding of low performers
- **Impact**: Quality issues will accumulate, RSV partners will lose trust
- **Recommendation**: Build lawyer performance dashboards (Q2 priority)
